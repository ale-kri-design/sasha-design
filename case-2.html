Problem

Problem definition

People experiencing migraines often struggle with understanding their own condition over time.

Many existing solutions either feel too complex when users are at their most vulnerable, or too simplistic to provide real value beyond logging data. This creates a gap between tracking information and actually learning from it.

→Understanding what triggers their attacks
→Tracking episodes consistently over time
→Reflecting on patterns in a meaningful way
→Sharing structured information with doctors
Research

Research approach

The goal was to understand not only what users track, but why, at which moments tracking breaks down, and how existing tools fail to support meaningful reflection.

→Desk research on migraine patterns and digital health tools
→Surveys with people suffering from migraines
→User interviews exploring triggers, habits, and pain points
→Competitive analysis of health and tracking apps
Research Outcome

Problem reframing

After synthesizing the research insights, it became clear that the core problem was not migraine tracking itself.

People are generally willing to log episodes, but logging alone does not help them understand their condition. The real challenge lies in making sense of fragmented data across time, triggers, and personal context - especially when users have limited cognitive and physical energy.

The problem, therefore, is not how to track migraines, but how to help users recognize patterns, reduce effort during vulnerable moments, and transform scattered information into clear and actionable understanding.

Key Insights

What I found

Trigger overload
Migraine triggers are numerous and often overlap, making it difficult for users to identify which factors genuinely affect their condition over time.
Invisible external factors
Environmental factors such as weather changes play a significant role for many users, yet are rarely tracked or meaningfully connected to migraine patterns in existing tools.
Screen avoidance during attacks
During a migraine attack, users avoid screens and complex interactions, making traditional tracking flows ineffective at the exact moment they are needed most.
Isolation and lack of feedback
Many users feel isolated in managing migraines and lack access to clear, up-to-date information or meaningful feedback about their condition.
Design Principles

Design principles

Reduce effort during vulnerable moments
Migraine attacks limit users' cognitive and physical energy. The experience prioritizes minimal input and avoids unnecessary interactions when users need simplicity most.
Prioritize patterns over isolated events
Single logs are less valuable than understanding trends over time. The interface focuses on summaries, correlations, and recurring patterns rather than individual entries.
Make data clearer
The overlap of triggers and data points requires clear structure that helps users interpret their condition rather than simply accumulate records.
Maintain a calm visual language
Visual restraint supports users during sensitive moments. The interface stays calm, low-contrast, and functional - especially important during photosensitivity.
Support reflection and data sharing
Tracking becomes truly valuable when it supports reflection and communication. The product helps users visualize patterns and prepare structured insights to share with doctors.

Product

Beginning of a migraine

One tap to start, minimal input during pain

→Reduce effort during vulnerable moments
→Maintain a calm visual language

When a migraine attack begins, users' cognitive abilities and visual sensitivity are reduced. At this moment, tracking should require as little effort as possible.

The interface focuses on a single primary action, with the option to add symptoms.

Symptoms and triggers

Quick selection, fully customisable

→Prioritize patterns
→Make data clearer

Migraine is influenced by multiple, often overlapping factors, but users struggle to understand which triggers are truly relevant for them.

Symptoms and triggers are organized in a separate section. This approach makes the data more reliable and easier to analyze over time.

Insights & patterns

Visual history of all episodes

→Prioritize patterns
→Support reflection and data sharing

Individual migraine entries offer limited value if taken out of context. The calendar section displays all past events in two different views to make it easier to explore personal data.

The insights section highlights recurring patterns, correlations, and trends - helping users understand what influences their migraines and supports informed reflection.

Reports

Doctor-ready insights at one tap

→Make data clearer
→Support reflection and data sharing

Many users struggle to clearly communicate their migraine history during medical visits. This product transforms personal tracking data into a structured report that can be reviewed or shared with a healthcare professional.

This reduces reliance on memory and turns personal data into actionable information.
