Florence is the most tourism-dense city in Italy by square kilometer. Its 5 km² Centro Storico, which is just 3.4% of the city's area, absorbs 76% of all overnight stays, while most Florentines live in the outer neighborhoods where tourist pressure is a fraction of what the centre endures.

Peak season runs from late April through mid-September, with July as the single most intense month — over 1.3 million overnight stays and 27 tourists for every resident in the historic centre. Crowds peak between July 10 and August 20.

The quiet season stretches from mid-November through February. Between December 27 and January 6, the city returns closest to its everyday rhythm — local bars fill again, streets move at a Florentine pace.
